# Deep-research report template

```markdown
# [Question, phrased as the title]

*Prepared [date] · [N] sources · Assumptions: [any scoping assumptions made]*

## Executive summary

[Direct answer to the question in 3–6 sentences. State the bottom line
first, then the strongest supporting points with citations. If the honest
answer is "it depends" or "contested", say so here, not in paragraph nine.]

## Findings

### [Sub-question 1]

[Prose findings. Every non-obvious statement cites a source: "...grew 34%
in 2025 [3]." Label key findings: **(Confidence: High)** — 2+ independent
sources; **(Confidence: Medium)** — single credible source or dated;
**(Confidence: Low)** — community anecdote, vendor claim, or estimate.]

### [Sub-question 2]

...

## Where sources disagree

- **[Contested claim]**: [Source A] says X [4]; [Source B] says Y [7].
  [Likely reason for the divergence — methodology, date, incentive.]

## Gaps and further research

- [Claim that remains single-source and would need verification before a
  high-stakes decision]
- [Angle not covered and why — paywalled, no data, out of time]

## Sources

1. [Title — Publisher, date. URL] *(primary)*
2. [Title — Publisher, date. URL] *(reporting)*
3. ...
```

Rules of thumb:

- Citation numbers are assigned in order of first use and never reused.
- Mark each source `primary` / `reporting` / `community` / `data` so the
  reader can weigh it.
- Time-sensitive figures carry their as-of date inline, not just in the
  source log.
- Keep the executive summary readable standalone — it is what gets pasted
  into the deck.
